## Organization at a Glance

mm-ARIEL spans **five main research themes** across ~30 repositories:

1. **Clinical Imaging Pipeline** — agentic processing of CT/MRI/X-ray data from the UNC Clinical Data Warehouse (CDW)
2. **Machine Unlearning & Privacy** — removing sensitive information from multimodal medical AI models
3. **3D Medical Image Segmentation** — foundation model adaptation and atlas-driven segmentation
4. **Multimodal Representation Learning** — MoE architectures, VLM fine-tuning, recurrent reasoning
5. **Data & Cohort Infrastructure** — OMOP CDW cohorts, UK Biobank, All of Us, TCGA

## About the Project

mm-ARIEL is part of the NIH Bridge2AI program. The project addresses a critical gap: lupus (SLE) disproportionately affects women of color, yet existing AI tools are trained on biased datasets. Our work aims to build multimodal representations that are both accurate and fair across demographic groups, using imaging data (X-rays, CT, MRI), clinical text, and structured EHR data from the UNC Health system.

- **Funding:** NIH Common Fund, Office of the Director (1OT2OD038045-01)
- **Lead institution:** UNC Chapel Hill
- **Collaborators:** UCSD, UNC, and others
